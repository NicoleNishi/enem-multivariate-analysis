# Multivariate Analysis of ENEM 2023 Student Performance

## Overview

This project explores educational and socioeconomic factors associated with student performance in the 2023 ENEM examination using a subset of 10,000 participants from São Paulo, Brazil.

Developed as part of a Multivariate Analysis course, the project applies a series of statistical and machine learning techniques to investigate relationships between academic achievement, socioeconomic conditions, family background, and school characteristics.

The study was conducted throughout a semester and organized into five stages, each focusing on different multivariate analysis methods.

---

## Objectives

* Investigate factors associated with ENEM performance.
* Identify socioeconomic variables related to educational outcomes.
* Build predictive and explanatory statistical models.
* Explore latent structures and patterns within educational data.
* Evaluate the impact of family and socioeconomic conditions on academic achievement.

---

## Dataset

The analyses were performed using data derived from the **ENEM 2023 Microdata** published by the Brazilian National Institute for Educational Studies and Research (INEP).

The dataset includes variables related to:

* Student performance in ENEM subjects
* Family income
* Parents' educational background
* School characteristics
* Access to technological resources
* Demographic information

## Key Findings

Some of the main insights obtained during the analyses include:

* Socioeconomic indicators consistently showed stronger associations with academic performance than several demographic variables.

- Family income and access to technological resources emerged as relevant predictors of student performance across multiple analyses.

* Parents' educational background was associated with better academic results.
* Logistic regression models achieved approximately 71% classification accuracy when predicting above-average essay performance.
* PCA and Factor Analysis revealed three major latent dimensions:

  * Academic Performance
  * Socioeconomic Status
  * Individual Profile / Maturity
* Socioeconomic variables were more effective than exam scores in distinguishing school types (public vs. private).

These findings reinforce the significant influence of socioeconomic conditions on educational achievement.
